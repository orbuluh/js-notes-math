# Learning Goals for Quantitative Finance

This roadmap focuses on the mathematical and statistical foundations needed for rigorous time-series modeling, market microstructure analysis, and portfolio decision-making.

## 1. Probability and Statistics

Build fluency in uncertainty quantification and risk measurement.

- Core metrics: expectation, variance, standard deviation, covariance, and correlation.
- Distributions: normal, pdf/cdf, plus heavy-tailed and skewed alternatives (Student's t, power-law behavior).
- Parameter calibration: method of moments for matching model moments to empirical data.
- Inference and testing: t-statistics, maximum likelihood estimation (MLE), and Newey-West standard errors under serial correlation.

## 2. Linear Algebra

Develop matrix-based tools for multi-asset data and factor/risk models.

- Matrix operations: transpose, inverse, pseudoinverse, and vectorized calculations.
- Eigen-decomposition: eigenvalues/eigenvectors for principal directions of risk.
- Dimensionality reduction: PCA and SVD for extracting latent statistical factors.

## 3. Time-Series Models and Econometrics

Model dynamics in returns, volatility, and cross-variable interactions.

- Autoregressive structure: AR(1) and related models for signal decay and persistence.
- Conditional heteroskedasticity: GARCH-family models for volatility clustering.
- Multivariate dependence: VAR for joint dynamics of returns, volume, and related variables.

## 4. Stochastic Processes and Dynamic Systems

Learn process-level models for high-frequency behavior and latent states.

- State-space models and Kalman filtering for hidden-state estimation.
- Point processes: Hawkes processes for self-exciting event clustering.
- Continuous-time dynamics: Brownian motion, Ornstein-Uhlenbeck processes, and Ito calculus.
- Market efficiency baseline: martingale intuition for price evolution.

## 5. Calculus and Optimization

Translate model outputs into executable portfolio and trading decisions.

- Mean-variance optimization (MVO) via quadratic programming.
- Constrained optimization with Lagrangian methods.
- Variational methods and Euler-Lagrange equations for continuous-time execution problems.
- Transform methods (Laplace/Fourier) for solving distributional and convolution-related equations.

## 6. Numerical Methods and Computation

Use computation when closed-form solutions are unavailable.

- Simulation and approximation for complex financial systems.
- Numerical optimization and gradient-based search.
- Solvers for matrix and differential equations (including Riccati-type equations).
- Robust implementation habits: stability checks, convergence diagnostics, and reproducibility.

## Milestone Outcome

By completing this roadmap, you should be able to:

- Build statistically defensible forecasting and risk models.
- Connect theoretical process models to observable market data.
- Implement optimization-based allocation or execution workflows.
- Validate model behavior with numerical experiments and diagnostics.
